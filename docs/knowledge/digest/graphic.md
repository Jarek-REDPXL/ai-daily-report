## Durable lessons (the compounding edge — evergreen takeaways we've taught)
<!-- promote a thread's conclusion here once it's a stable, reusable principle. -->
- **Direct, don't re-shoot.** The leverage in AI video is no longer 'generate a clip from a prompt' — it's editing footage you already have: change the angle, the background, or the grade from a reference, with keyframe edits that track a subject across frames. Reach for tools that EDIT (preserve what you didn't touch) over tools that regenerate from scratch, and keep the work in the file where the rest of the creative lives.
- **For design output, prompt the model as a structured brief, not a sentence:** declaring copy strings, exact hex palette, and element positions as data is what makes AI image gen repeatable, templatable, and free of garbled in-image text — the difference between a one-off picture and a production pipeline.
- **Demand editable formats from AI, not flat pixels:** an AI logo/icon delivered as a PNG is a dead end — you can't recolour, rescale cleanly, or hand it to a dev. Reach for tools that output native SVG (vector) so the asset stays editable in Figma/Illustrator and becomes a real brand component, not a screenshot.
- **Train the brand once; keep the product reference in the loop:** for repeatable on-brand AI creative, train a custom model on your best work (so 'on-brand' stops being re-prompted every time) AND use a reference-aware model (Nano Banana 2 et al.) that holds the real product's details across edits — the combination is what turns AI from cleanup-work into a brief-in/options-out pipeline. Pair it with a reusable Claude brief that encodes your design principles.
